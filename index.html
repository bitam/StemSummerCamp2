About Hoops

Modern hoops are great for fitness, dance and just plain fun for all. Unlike the hoops sod in stores, these hoops are handmade and designed to be comfortable for adults.



How does the weight and diameter effect hooping?

  • How does the diameter effect the hoop's speed?
    • The larger the diameter of a hoop is, the slower it is. So, larger hoops require fewer nudges to maintain. Average hoop diameter for beginners is 41-43".
    • The smaller the diameter of a hoop is, the faster it is. So, smaller hoops require more spins to to maintain on body movements.
  • Are thicker and heavier hoops easier to maintain?
    • Heavier hoops have more momentum.

      I've often been asked "Are thes hoops weighted?"

      The answer is No.
      The tubing used is thicker manifesting in added weight. Heavier hoops have more mementum. You can think of a heavier hoop being similar to a heaver bowling ball. If you've ever tried your hand at different weights, you notice that the heavier bowling balls maintain their course much more easily.

      Beginners generally find 160 psi tubing to be comfortable.

    • Lighter hoops are easier to spin, espeically for out-of-body body movements, similar to ther modern performance art of Poi.

      More advanceds hoopers and those who want to incorporate out-of-body hand movements prefer the lighter 100 psi tubing.
